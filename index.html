Abstract

Advancing towards artificial superintelligence requires rich and intelligent perceptual capabilities[cite: 47]. A critical frontier in this pursuit is overcoming the limited spatial understanding of Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), where geometry information is essential[cite: 48]. Existing methods often address this by rigidly injecting geometric signals into every input, while ignoring their necessity and adding computation overhead[cite: 49].

Contrary to this paradigm, our framework endows the model with an awareness of perceptual insufficiency, empowering it to autonomously engage geometric features in reasoning when 2D cues are deemed insufficient[cite: 50]. To achieve this, we first introduce an independent geometry input channel to the model architecture and conduct alignment training[cite: 51]. Subsequently, we curate a dedicated spatial-aware supervised fine-tuning dataset to activate the model's latent internal cues[cite: 52, 53]. Experiments across multiple spatial reasoning benchmarks demonstrate significant spatial gains without compromising 2D visual reasoning capabilities[cite: 54].

Methodology

GeoSense dynamically modulates its reliance on geometry input[cite: 168]. The training framework consists of two main stages:

  • Independent Geometry Adaptation: We treat 3D geometry as an independent, on-demand modality rather than fusing it via element-wise summation[cite: 269]. Both visual and geometric encoders extract features, which are then projected via dedicated MLP modules to a shared embedding space[cite: 271, 272].
  • Activating Internal Spatial Awareness: During inference, the model dynamically makes an "Internal Sense Decision" based on the 2D visual and textual prompt[cite: 298]. For tasks requiring high spatial precision, the model is trained to trigger a signal (e.g., the <vggt> token) to request geometric embeddings[cite: 299, 373]. If the signal is not triggered, the channel is suppressed to maintain 2D visual reasoning purity[cite: 377].

Key Results

Our proposed model significantly outperforms the baseline Qwen2.5-VL-3B on spatial reasoning benchmarks under comparable training data scales[cite: 559].

  • Spatial Reasoning: GeoSense achieves SOTA performance across spatial reasoning tasks like VSI-Bench and MindCube[cite: 92]. For example, on the challenging BLINK benchmark, our method achieves a remarkable gain (+35.4) compared to the baseline[cite: 564, 657].
  • General Visual Reasoning: The model maintains robust general-purpose reasoning[cite: 174]. By dynamically reverting to standard 2D and textual embedding combinations, it effectively mitigates cross-modal interference typically caused by redundant geometric features in non-spatial contexts[cite: 561, 562].
